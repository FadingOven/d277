About

Montana became a state in the year 1889. Known for picturesque views, plentiful wildlife, and a sense of adventure. The state is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts as their is plenty of opportunity for hunting, fishing, camping, biking, hiking, skiing, and whatever else you can imagine!

Population

The current population of Montana sits at 1,005,141 citizens as of the 2022 state Census. The majority of residents reside in Yellowstone County, specifically the city of Billings. Billings has the largest population, however the state capitol is located in Helena. Two other popular cities for tourism are Bozeman and Kalispell.

Governor

The state is governed by Greg Gianforte (R). Gianforte won the state governor election of 2020.

State animals

The state has a state animal, a state bird, and a state fish. The state animal is the grizzly bear. The state bird is the western meadowlark, and the state fish is the cutthroat trout.

State Motto and nicknames

Montana's official motto is Oro y Plata (Gold and Silver). The state has many nicknames such as the Treasure State and Big Sky Country.
